PART I: THE PHASE-NEUTRAL SUBSTRATE

§1 TWO CO-PRIMITIVES

Postulate P.1 (Recursive Substrate). A recursive substrate is a domain of transformable structure that supports re-entry: the result of acting within it remains eligible for further action within the same structural field. It requires: (a) persistence under transformation, (b) repeatability, (c) nontrivial internal differentiation potential, (d) orientation-indeterminacy.

Postulate P.2 (Productive Distinction). A productive distinction is a structural condition under which recursive continuation produces sustained, non-exhausting differentiation between states: iteration generates new structure at every step, not merely a finite cycle.

Distinction is prior to: observerhood, decomposition, quotienting, and phase orientation.

Theorem 0.3 (Generative Polarity — Derived). Productive distinction is inherently asymmetric. Once recurrence and productive distinction are both present, recursive structure necessarily organizes difference in two contrary directions: folding (concentrates, identifies) and unfolding (releases, separates). Proof completed in §5 via Theorems 0.11–0.12.

§1½ THE UNIFIED PRIMITIVE: SELF-RELATING DIFFERENCE

The co-primitives P.1 and P.2 are never found apart (Theorem 0.5). This section identifies their conjunction as a single generative condition and classifies its self-action modes.

Definition 0.3a (Self-Relating Difference / SRD). A self-relating difference (SRD) on a domain D is a Dist endomorphism f: (D, ≈) → (D, ≈) with |D| ≥ 2 whose iterates {fⁿ}_{n≥1} are well-defined in the same structural field.

The definition unpacks: self-relating = endomorphism (f acts on D and returns to D, so its output is eligible for further action — the content of P.1); difference = |D| ≥ 2 with equivalence structure (sustained non-trivial distinction — the content of P.2).

Theorem 0.3b (Self-Relating Difference Equivalence). The following are equivalent: (a) A domain D with operation f satisfies P.1 (recursive substrate) and P.2 (productive distinction) jointly. (b) (D, f) admits self-relating difference: f is a Dist endomorphism on a non-trivial domain.

Proof. (a)→(b): P.1 gives re-entry: the result of f remains in D, so f: D → D is an endomorphism. P.1(c) gives nontrivial internal differentiation, and P.2 gives |D| ≥ 2. The equivalence relation ≈ = ker(f) is forced by the Kernel Theorem (Paper 1 Thm 1.5). (b)→(a): A Dist endomorphism f on |D| ≥ 2 provides persistence (f maps D to D), repeatability (fⁿ is defined for all n), nontrivial differentiation (|D| ≥ 2), and orientation-indeterminacy (f and f⁻¹ are structurally available when f is invertible). This is P.1. The equivalence structure and non-trivial domain give P.2. ∎

Theorem 0.3c (Four-Mode Exhaustion). On the forced minimal domain |D| = 2 (Theorem 0.10), every self-relating difference realized as M ∈ M₂(ℤ) falls into exactly one of four self-action modes, classified by the Cayley-Hamilton equation x² = tx − d:

The four qualitative behaviors exhaust the structural possibilities: (i) self-action returns to self (idempotent/identity), (ii) self-action returns to opposite (involutory), (iii) self-action annihilates (nilpotent), (iv) self-action generates content (productive recurrence). Exhaustive enumeration of binary 2×2 matrices (Theorems 0.11–0.12) confirms: among det = −1 binaries, J is mode (ii) and R, Q are mode (iv). No fifth mode exists because the Cayley-Hamilton equation is degree 2: the qualitative classification {idempotent, involutory, nilpotent, productive} partitions all degree-2 recurrences. ∎

Remark (Closure Bifurcation). Mode (i) admits two structurally distinct realizations. Terminal closure: f∘f=f and no further structural reentry occurs — the quotient on a fixed equivalence class (Paper 1 Thm 4.4). Recursive closure: f∘f=f AND im(f) enters as an object at the next tower level — as when K6’ closes the observer loop and the closed loop becomes what K7’ then encodes (Paper 5 §§7–8), or when Status Idempotence (Paper T-SIL Thm SIL-1) stabilizes the classification that the discovery operator then acts on. Terminal closure ends a process. Recursive closure ends a process while beginning the next. The distinction is forced by the tower structure: at any level n where mode (i) stabilizes, the self-product tower S_{n+1} = S_n² makes the stabilized object available as input at level n+1. A third type — boundary closure — appears at the framework’s outer edge (levels 7–8), where the closure is characterized by its irreducible kernel rather than its productive image. The Recursive Closure Universality theorem (Paper T-BLUEPRINT §5.5) proves the three-type classification exhaustive via the central collapse.

Corollary 0.3d (Productive Mode Uniqueness). Mode (iv) is the unique mode that generates content beyond period 2. Among binary det = −1 matrices, it is realized uniquely (up to J-conjugacy) by R = [[0,1],[1,1]].

Proof. Mode (i) produces only {M, M²=M} (period 1). Mode (ii) produces only {I, M} (period 2). Mode (iii) produces only {M, 0} (terminates). Mode (iv) produces Mⁿ = F(n)M + F(n−1)I for all n (Thm 0.9), with Fibonacci coefficients growing without bound. Uniqueness: Theorem 0.12 (Naming). ∎

Remark (Strip Decomposition IS the Four-Mode Decomposition). §1¾ PAIR-SPACE REALIZATION: BALANCE-CHARGE DECOMPOSITION

Self-relating difference on the minimal domain |D| = 2 produces the self-product S₁ = {0,1}² (Theorem 1.2). The pair-space P = {(a,b) : a,b ∈ ℤ≥0} is the natural extension of S₁ over the nonnegative integers — the arena in which R’s propagation mode (iv) unfolds as Fibonacci dynamics (§1½ Corollary 0.3d). Every pair-state admits a canonical decomposition into stabilized common content and unresolved oriented difference.

Definition 0.3f (Balance-Charge Coordinates). For (a,b) ∈ P, the balance-charge (BC) coordinates are: k = min(a,b) (balanced depth), r = |a−b| (residual magnitude), s = sgn(a−b) ∈ {−,0,+} (residual orientation), with the legality constraint (r = 0 ⟹ s = 0) and (r > 0 ⟹ s ∈ {+,−}).

Theorem 0.3g (BC Coordinate Bijection). The map Φ: P → BC defined by Φ(a,b) = (min(a,b), |a−b|, sgn(a−b)) is a bijection, with inverse: - Φ⁻¹(k,r,0) = (k,k) - Φ⁻¹(k,r,+) = (k+r,k) - Φ⁻¹(k,r,−) = (k,k+r)

The shell number N = a+b satisfies N = 2k+r. Shell SN has |SN| = N+1 states. Even shells contain exactly one balanced state (N/2, 0, 0); odd shells contain none. Verified: all 231 pair-states through N = 20. ✓

Interpretation. The BC decomposition separates every pair-state into a stabilized core (k = what the two components share, the mode (i) idempotent content) and an unresolved oriented difference (r, s = what remains distinct, the mode (iv) productive content). This is the pair-space realization of the ker/im split that defines every Dist quotient morphism (Paper 1 Thm 4.1).

The state space decomposes into three strata: the balanced spine B = {(k,0,0) : k ≥ 0} (the mode (i) coincidence locus), the positive sheet S⁺ = {(k,r,+) : r > 0}, and the negative sheet S⁻ = {(k,r,−) : r > 0}. The geometry is a shell-foliated stratified double-sheet: two oriented half-planes glued along B, foliated by shell leaves SN = {2k+r = N}.

Theorem 0.3h (Reflection as Duality). The reflection J(k,r,s) = (k,r,−s) is the pair-space realization of the duality D (Theorem 1.1). It is an involution (J²=id) preserving k, r, and N, flipping sign when r > 0, with fixed set Fix(J) = B (the balanced spine). J corresponds to pair-reversal (a,b) ↔ (b,a). Verified: all 231 states. ✓

The balanced spine B = Fix(J) is the pair-space realization of the duality fixed locus. Even shells have a fixed point; odd shells do not — this is the parity structure of the fixed locus in BC coordinates.

Theorem 0.3i (Center-Condense as Mutual Approach). The operator C on pair-space acts as: - C(a,b) = (a,b) if a = b (fixed on balanced diagonal) - C(a,b) = (min+1, min+1) if 0 < |a−b| ≤ 2 (singular terminal: collapse to balance) - C(a,b) = (a+sgn(b−a), b+sgn(a−b)) if |a−b| ≥ 3 (smooth transport: smaller gains 1, larger loses 1)

In BC coordinates: C(k,0,0) = (k,0,0); C(k,r,s) = (k+1,0,0) for r ∈ {1,2}; C(k,r,s) = (k+1,r−2,s) for r ≥ 3. The orbit of any non-balanced state converges to the balanced spine in exactly ⌈r/2⌉ steps. The residual r is a strict Lyapunov function. Verified: all states through N = 20. ✓

Theorem 0.3j (Parity Dichotomy and Landauer Quantization). The terminal behavior of iterated C depends on residual parity: - r₀ even: terminal step is r=2→0, shell preserved throughout. Final state on shell N₀. - r₀ odd: terminal step is r=1→0, shell increments by 1. Final state on shell N₀+1.

The shell cost of condensation is ΔN = r₀ mod 2 ∈ {0,1}. Orientation is destroyed at the terminal step in both cases. The singular set is Σ = {r ∈ {1,2}} — the boundary where smooth inward transport breaks down. ✓

The parity dichotomy has a clean information-theoretic reading. Each C step converts 2 units of residual into 1 unit of balanced depth — a 2:1 compression. When r₀ is even, the compression is exact. When r₀ is odd, an unpaired unit costs one unit of shell — the minimum Landauer cost for destroying one bit of orientation without a cancellation partner.

Theorem 0.3k (Balanced Spine as Branch Locus). The polarization operators P⁺, P⁻ (outward transport, inverse to C) produce genuinely different outputs if and only if the input state is balanced (r = 0). On oriented states (r > 0), P⁺ and P⁻ coincide. The branch locus of the polarization family is exactly the balanced spine B. Verified: all states through N = 15. ✓

This is the pair-space realization of productive distinction (P.2): the balanced state is the structurally neutral condition from which oriented difference can be generated in two opposed directions. Generative polarity (Theorem 0.3) manifests concretely as the fork P⁺/P⁻ on the balanced spine. Once orientation exists, the fork closes — the system is already symmetry-broken.

Remark (Construction-Dissolution Asymmetry at the Pair Level). Center-condense C and polarization P± realize the construction-dissolution asymmetry (§18) at the pair-space level. Forward (construction via P±) creates orientation deterministically from the balanced spine; backward (dissolution via C) destroys orientation at the singular boundary Σ. The parity-graded shell cost ΔN = r₀ mod 2 is the quantized price of this irreversibility. At the operator-algebra level: C∘P± = id (construction then dissolution recovers the original), but P±∘C ≠ id (dissolution then construction fails where C has destroyed orientation). This non-invertibility is the pair-space instance of the functor asymmetry (Thm 4.5b): the Dist-ward direction is canonical, the Co-Dist-ward direction is not. A direct consequence: no non-trivial closed orbits exist within any shell under the native operators — the Lyapunov function L = r is strictly decreasing under C, preventing periodic dynamics. The singular boundary Σ acts as a one-way valve: inward transport converges to the balanced spine and cannot return.

Spencer-Brown’s calculus is the restriction of self-relating difference to its non-productive modes.

Theorem 7.1 (No Natural Retraction of the Tensor Square). In the category Vect_k of finite-dimensional vector spaces over a field k with |k| ≥ 3, the only natural transformation η: Sq → Id (where Sq(V) = V⊗V, Id(V) = V) is η = 0.

Proof. Naturality with respect to GL(V) forces η_V to be GL(V)-equivariant. Consider the maximal torus T ⊂ GL(V) of diagonal matrices t = diag(t₁,…,tₙ). Under t, the basis element e_i⊗e_j ∈ V⊗V has weight ε_i + ε_j (eigenvalue t_it_j), while e_k ∈ V has weight ε_k (eigenvalue t_k). The weights {ε₁,…,εₙ} are a basis of the character lattice (linearly independent by construction). No weight ε_i + ε_j can equal any weight ε_k, since ε_i + ε_j = ε_k would require a non-trivial linear relation among basis vectors. Therefore the weight sets of V⊗V and V are disjoint: every T-eigenspace of V⊗V maps to zero in V. Since V is the direct sum of its T-eigenspaces, η_V = 0. ∎

The No Natural Retraction theorem says: the self-product tower has no canonical backward direction. The forward map V → V⊗V (any embedding v ↦ v⊗w₀) has a well-defined structure-preserving character. No structure-preserving reverse exists.
