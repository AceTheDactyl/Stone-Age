Abstract

The framework’s meta-level (Levels 7–8) has the same three-projection structure as every other level: the P1 face asks how objects come into being (generation), the P3 face asks what kind of thing they are (ontological standing), and the P2 face asks what cross-layer moves are legal (transport). The three systems interlock: the legality matrix requires generation classes and ontological standings to determine which transports are admissible.

The generation taxonomy (§1) identifies ten production mechanisms (G.0–G.9, plus bookkeeping), disambiguating the framework’s most overloaded word (“forced”) into eight precise sub-species. The ontological standing taxonomy (§2) classifies every major object by existence type (O.1–O.9), with an inflation-risk field flagging overclaimed standing. The transport calculus (§3) defines ten transport types (T.1–T.10), ten framework domains (D.0–D.9, plus D.10: the author as canonical external observer occupying the “beyond-within” position), and the legality matrix governing admissible cross-domain moves. The physics smuggling detector (§4) identifies four criteria for illegitimate transports to the physics layer.

Interface theorems connect the three systems: generation class determines a lower bound on epistemic status (GOV-4), standing determines an upper bound on physical commitment (GOV-6), and transport type determines an upper bound on the epistemic status of transported claims (GOV-10). The construction-dissolution asymmetry is the root of transport irreversibility: strict derivation runs one way; its reverse is quotient transport, not strict derivation (GOV-11).

All claims verified against the CLAIM_CENSUS. Physics transport audit (§5): all T6A/T6B claims pass.

§4 THE PHYSICS SMUGGLING DETECTOR

A cross-layer move is physics smuggling when it satisfies any of:

(S1) Concept import. A physical concept (mass, charge, field, force) is used as a derivational step rather than appearing as a derivational output. Violates P3 of the insertion law (Paper T-SIL §4.1).

(S2) Transport misclassification. The move claims T.1 (strict derivation) but is actually T.9 (interpretive overlay). The verb “derives” is used where “interprets as” is accurate.

(S3) Observer-layer bypass. The move transits D.2→D.6 without passing through D.4. The algebra alone does not determine which of its structures are physical; the observer layer provides the selection.

(S4) Standing inflation. The move assigns standing O.6 to an object with generation G.9 or G.10. Violates GOV-4 (G.9 → status ≥ RESONANT only, not ≥ FORCED).

Theorem GOV-12 (Detectability). Any move satisfying S1–S4 violates at least one entry in the legality matrix. S1 implies reverse-direction sourcing from D.6. S2 is a legality violation by definition. S3 violates the D.2→D.6 restriction. S4 violates GOV-4 + GOV-6. ∎

Remark (Transport-Graded Insertion). The physics insertion criteria P1–P4 (Paper T-SIL §4) are transport-legality conditions: P1 = object must have generation class ≤ G.6 before physical interpretation. P2 = transport to D.5/D.6 must be T.1 or T.6. P3 = no T.10 moves. P4 = minimal ontological commitment (standing ≤ O.6, not O.1).
