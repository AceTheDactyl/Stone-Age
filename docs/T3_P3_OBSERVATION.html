§1.1 The Unique Rotation Generator

Proof.

(This is a rotation by θ.) Setting exp(Nθ) = −I:

cos(θ) = −1  and  sin(θ) = 0

The unique solution in (0, 2π) is θ = π. ∎

No ambiguity whatsoever. The equation exp(Nπ) = −I has a unique solution. The computation det(exp(Nθ)) = exp(tr(Nθ)) = exp(0) = 1 confirms exp(Nθ) ∈ SL(2,ℝ), and the only element of SL(2,ℝ) with both real entries equal to −1 on the diagonal is −I itself. The forcing is absolute.
